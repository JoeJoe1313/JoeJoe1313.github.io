Meta’s TRImodal Brain Encoder (TRIBE)

Published: Thu 21 August 2025

In Quick Takes.

tags: ai

Researchers from Meta have developed TRIBE (TRImodal Brain Encoder), the first deep encoding pipeline trained across text + audio + video, multiple cortical areas, and individuals, winning 1st place in Algonauts 2025.

TRIBE fuses features from Llama-3.2-3B (text), Wav2Vec2-BERT (audio), and V-JEPA-2 (video) with a temporal Transformer and subject embeddings to predict 1,000-parcel fMRI responses to naturalistic videos. The architecture is available in Figure 1 bloew.

Why it’s cool

  ✅ Late fusion of strong unimodal FMs > unimodal or VL-only baselines

  ✅ Learns shared dynamics via subject embeddings

  ✅ Robust to missing inputs with modality dropout

  ✅ Generalizes OOD: cartoons, documentaries, even silent B&W

Key results

  🥇 1st on Algonauts 2025 public leaderboard

  📊 Captures ~54% of explainable variance on average

  🔬 Trained/evaluated on 80+ hours of fMRI per participant

  ⚡ Up to ~30% higher encoding scores in associative cortices vs the best unimodal model
